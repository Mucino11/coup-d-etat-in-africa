The motivations behind the coups

Delving further into the motivations driving these coups, it becomes clear that France's economic interests hold a central position. Mineral-rich regions like Niger with its uranium, Gabon with its crude oil, and Guinea with its bauxite reserves, serve as significant sources of economic influence for France. This economic interdependence establishes a fragile equilibrium, and any alteration in power dynamics can precipitate political instability.

Conclusion

In conclusion, many of the recent coups in Africa can be traced back to a prevailing anti-French sentiment among the inhabitants of Francophone nations. A significant portion of the population feels that France has exploited them for an extended period, prompting a desire for change. This sentiment often translates into a desire to oust governments aligned with France.

While some coup leaders genuinely seek changes that would benefit their people, others may exploit this anti-French sentiment as a pretext to seize power and perpetuate the existing status quo.
